Birria Tacos

Description

Birria is a Mexican dish from the state of Jalisco. It is a traditional stew made from a combination of chili pepper-based goat meat adobo, garlic, cumin, bay leaves, and thyme, cooked over low heat. Birria is slow stewed in a pot to create tender, juicy meat that shreds easily.

The recipe I use is slightly less traditional, as it used ingredients that will be available at most supermarkets in the United States. It requires patience to make it, but it is definitely worth it!

Ingredients

  • Beef(1 lb Cab sirloin and 1.5 lb beef shank)
  • 3 dried guajillo peppers
  • 1 can chipotle peppers in adobo
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 5 cloves garlic
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 onion(Yellow or white)
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 5 cloves
  • 1/2 cup crushed tomatoes
  • 1/4 cup vinegar
  • cilantro(for garnish)
  • lime juice(preferably fresh)

Steps

  1. Bring a large pot of water to a boil and then remove from heat. Soak dried peppers for 15 minutes.
  2. Cube cab sirloin, then season both cab and shank with salt and pepper.
  3. Once the peppers have soaked thoroughly, add them to blender along with vinegar, chipotles in adobo, crushed tomatoes, garlic, oregano, smoked paprika, and cumin. once combined, pour marinade over meat and set in fridge for 2 hour up to overnight.
  4. Set instant pot or large stew pot to medium heat and saute onion with 1-2 tbsp oil for 6-8 minutes.
  5. Add meats, marinade, bay leaves, cinnamon stick, and cloves to the pot. Cover with chicken broth and set stove to low heat for 4-6 hours.
  6. After simmering is done, remove meat, shred, and set aside. Discard the bones.
  7. Serve with tortillas, cilantro, diced onion, mexican cheese, and lime. Enjoy!
